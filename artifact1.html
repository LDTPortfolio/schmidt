S. 1.1. Creating:

Full Working Website

by Olivia Schmidt 4th May 2023 EME 6207

“Candidates demonstrate the ability to create instructional materials and learning environments using a variety of systems approaches.

For the capstone project of EME6207, we were tasked to create a full working website – a demonstration of the web design skills we had taken away from the course. The parameters for our site were that it must be fully-operational, and hand-coded with HTML and CSS. I created this website (Link Here), which also allowed me to create the basis for the E-portfolio site you’re visiting now. The design and development process for my original website took several weeks of familiarizing myself with web design principles, watching HTML/CSS tutorial videos on YouTube, consulting friends who’ve created similar projects, and communicating with my professor when I found myself at a design or technical impasse. With my weeks-long design and development process, I believe my efforts align with the systems approach to instructional design – emphasizing a methodical and iterative process in creating learning environments.

Instructional Designer Competencies and Skills:
  • Select and construct appropriate interactions and user control techniques
  • Use a variety of authoring and/or software applications packages
  • Identify relevant resources, constraints, and context of the development and delivery environments
