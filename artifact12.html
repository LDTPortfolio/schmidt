S. 4.3. Reflection on Practice:

Individual Front-end Analysis

by Olivia Schmidt 10th September 2023 EME 6284

“Candidates analyze and interpret data and artifacts and reflect on the effectiveness of the design, development and implementation of technology-supported instruction and learning to enhance their professional growth.”

In my individual front-end analysis (FEA), prior to when my peers and I were grouped together and our FEA ended up becoming the subject of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI), I had envisioned a training that diverged the topic of DEI. In my own FEA, I wrote of a training targeted toward teenagers that would develop their media literacy skills. In this training, students would be empowered to employ critical thinking and media literacy skills to better navigate the digital world effectively in our present era of information overload. The deliverables for this assignment were identifying the context and need for this instruction, the required form and the content needed for my target audience, methods of data collection, a target audience analysis, etc. In my reflection on the effectiveness of the design, development, and implementation of such a proposed training, I believe I enhanced my own professional growth by gaining a heightened awareness of the critical role media literacy plays in the lives of today's teenagers.
