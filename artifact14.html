S. 5.3. Assessing/Evaluating:

Practical Understanding of the Literature

by Olivia Schmidt 21st July 2023 EDF 6481

“Candidates apply formal inquiry strategies in assessing and evaluating processes and resources for learning and performance.”

This artifact was our final project for Foundations of Educational Research; we were tasked to analyze a research paper coming out of Rutgers University called “State School Finance Inequities and the Limits of Pursuing Teacher Equity through Departmental Regulation”. In this analysis, we would take the practical knowledge we’d learned throughout the course: being able to demonstrate an understanding of scholarly research reports, correctly interpret the results said research reports and their components, and be able to discuss the meaning and application of their conclusions.In this final analysis, we had to answer questions directly related to the content of the scholarly report, such as “from figures A, B, and C, what can you say about Florida spending on students? . . . make an individual statement about each of the three figures, or a global statement that explains all three.” The completion of this project directly relates to the standard of “apply[ing] formal inquiry strategies in assessing and evaluating processes and resources for learning and performance” because these kind of research skills are necessary to also investigate, analyze, and judge various aspects of educational processes.
