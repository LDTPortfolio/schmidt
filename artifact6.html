S. 2.4. Managing:

A Mini-Course on Serious Games

by Olivia Schmidt 30th April 2023 EME 6457

“Candidates manage appropriate technological processes and resources to provide supportive learning communities, create flexible and diverse learning environments, and develop and demonstrate appropriate content pedagogy.”

In this artifact, we were tasked to design a mini-course that would take place over two weeks. I was able to apply the theoretical knowledge I took from EME6457 and combine it with my existing technical skills to produce a mini-course through the Canvas LMS. I chose to develop my course on the subject of serious games – games whose main purpose deviates from entertainment. As the sole course designer and instructor for A Mini Course on Serious Games, I developed 7 modules, with the first module being the course introduction and the last module being a course evaluation for students to complete. Several modules were designed to encourage collaboration and develop supportive learning communities among a total of four students in my course; for example, in module 5, students had a social debate on the future of serious games, and where this burgeoning industry is heading. Overall, this artifact displayed that I have demonstrated how to facilitate the design course process and create a learning environment that demonstrates designated pedagogy.
