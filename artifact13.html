S. 5.1. Theoretical Foundations:

Big Data Final Project

by Olivia Schmidt 17th July 2023 EME 6356

“Candidates demonstrate foundational knowledge of the contribution of research to the past and current theory of educational communications and technology.”

In this culminating project for Big Data & Learning Analytics, I wrote on the role of socioeconomic status (SES) in the identification of gifted students and its implications for educational equity. My project aimed to determine whether socioeconomic factors influence the accurate assessment and identification of potentially gifted students using data collected from the Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) and the U.S. Census Bureau. I examined indicators for giftedness and SES at various geographic levels and in the context of ethnicity/race and English Language Learner (ELL) status. Ultimately, I sought to understand the issues of underrepresentation in gifted programs and how this impacts equitable access to these programs. My paper focuses on the identification of gifted students from low SES backgrounds and their representation in gifted programs, highlighting disparities among different demographic groups. The goal of my project was to shed light on the complex factors affecting gifted student identification and to address these issues to promote more equitable access to gifted education programs.
