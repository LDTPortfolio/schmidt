S. 3.2. Using:

Final IDP/PLAN

by Olivia Schmidt 1st October 2023 EDF 6284

“Candidates make professionally sound decisions in selecting appropriate processes and resources to provide optimal conditions for learning based on principles, theories, and effective practices.”

In this group project, I worked with three others to develop an instructional design plan to aid in the development of our Computer-Based Training (CBT). We created this IDP for a range of individuals working in various sectors, from corporate, educational, government and public sector, etc. – the subject of our training being diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI). Our final IDP outlines the need for such training, the target audience we aimed to address, the performance objectives for our audience, instructional activities and strategies to be included, the kind of assessments for students, the software we would use to develop our training, etc. After we agreed on the plan, we each designated a section we’d contribute to the document; I worked on the “Need”, “Target Audience”, and “Assessment” sections. Over the course of the semester, our group would meet several times to make adjustments to our prototype and refer back to this original IDP document.

Instructional Designer Competencies and Skills:
  • Develop a needs assessment/analysis plan and instrument
  • Select the target audience and make appropriate adjustments to achieve project goals
  • Develop a target audience profile
  • Identify how the courseware will be used in the curriculum
  • State a rationale for the selection of the resources and constraints of the development and delivery environments chose
  • State an objective in performance terms that reflect the intent of instruction
